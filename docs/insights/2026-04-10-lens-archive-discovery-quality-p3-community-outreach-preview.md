## 1. Outreach Goal

The goal of this outreach is not broad awareness. It is to test whether a narrow, bridge-shaped discovery promise produces specific, high-signal replies from creators with recurring visibility needs.

The promise under test is:

> Help the right viewers repeatedly discover the work, then move them into a more trusted evaluation and contact path.

Success signals:

- replies define a specific viewer type
- replies interpret the idea as qualified discovery, not generic exposure
- replies indicate willingness to change channel allocation or publishing behavior

Failure signals:

- replies collapse into "more exposure"
- replies treat the idea as another portfolio or another feed
- distrust of platform-mediated discovery dominates the response

## 5. Draft Post: Photrio

### Title

Not more exposure, but the right viewers repeatedly seeing the work?

### Body

I am trying to validate a very narrow photography-discovery hypothesis and would appreciate a harsh read from people here.

This is not about building another portfolio site, another general social feed, or another likes/followers game.

The narrower question is:

If a photography product could help the right viewers repeatedly discover your work, and then move them into a more trusted path such as your own site, profile, or direct contact flow, would that actually matter?

By "right viewers" I do not mean "more people." I mean things like:

- the kinds of clients you actually want
- repeat collaborators
- serious peers or curators
- people with a real reason to look deeper

If you are willing to answer, I would especially value these four points:

1. your city or primary region
2. the kind of photography you do
3. who you most want to be seen by this year
4. whether that kind of discovery would change where or how you publish work

If your answer is "no, this still sounds like another vague platform promise," that is also useful.

## 6. Draft Post: DPReview

### Title

Would repeatable discovery by the right viewers matter more than broader exposure?

### Body

I am exploring a narrow product hypothesis for working photographers and want to test whether the underlying problem is real.

The idea is not "another photography platform" in the broad sense.

The narrower claim is:

For some photographers, the problem is not that nobody sees the work. The problem is that the right viewers do not see it repeatedly enough for that attention to turn into serious follow-up.

So the question is:

If a product could improve repeatable discovery among the people who actually matter to your work, and then hand them off into a more trusted place such as your own site or direct contact flow, would that be valuable?

If helpful, please answer with:

1. your city or main market
2. what kind of work you do
3. who the "right viewers" actually are for you
4. whether that would change how you allocate effort between Instagram, your site, Behance, referrals, or other channels

I am explicitly not asking whether "more exposure" is good. I am trying to separate qualified discovery from generic visibility.

## 7. Draft Post: Reddit `r/photographybusiness`

### Title

For your business, is the problem really "more exposure" or "the right people not seeing your work often enough"?

### Body

I am testing a narrow hypothesis and would love blunt responses from people here.

This is not a portfolio-builder question and not a social-growth question.

The narrower idea is:

Some photographers may not need broader exposure as much as they need the right people to keep seeing the work often enough to trigger a real next step.

If that were true, a useful product would not just get more eyeballs. It would help the right viewers repeatedly discover the work and then move them into a more trusted path like your own site, a profile, or direct contact.

If you are willing to answer, please include:

1. your city or market
2. your photography niche
3. who you actually want to be seen by
4. whether a promise like this would change where you put your effort

If your answer is still "no, the real problem is just more visibility or stronger referrals," that is equally useful.
